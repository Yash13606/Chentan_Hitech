## Product Purpose

A B2B digital quotation and industrial procurement platform for Chetan Hi-Tech, a commercial equipment supplier. The platform is not an e-commerce store. It is a product discovery, lead capture, and structured quotation request system. Clients browse equipment, build a quotation cart, and submit an RFQ. The sales team receives structured leads and generates official quotation PDFs. All final transactions happen offline.

## Target Users

**External (Procurement Buyers)**
- Facility managers, procurement officers, project engineers at: Hotels & Resorts, Hospitals, Defence organizations (Navy, Army canteens), Corporate campuses, Industrial facilities, Cloud kitchens, Educational institutions, Marine and shipyard operations
- They are making large, long-lead infrastructure decisions. They want precision, credibility, and a clear path to a formal commercial offer, not a shopping cart with a checkout button.

**Internal (Sales and Admin Team)**
- Sales team members who manage inbound leads and convert inquiries to closed deals
- Quotation managers who generate official PDFs
- Admins who manage the product catalog, users, and platform settings

## Brand Personality

Editorial authority. Industrial gravitas. Warm approachability. The platform should feel like a precision-engineered catalog from a trusted supplier, not a startup SaaS product. Think: a well-designed equipment specification journal, not a marketplace. Confident, specific, never hype-driven.

## Anti-References

- Generic SaaS landing pages with feature grids and testimonial carousels
- Consumer e-commerce aesthetics (price badges, cart icons, ratings stars)
- Cold industrial blue/grey tech interfaces
- Startup-warm gradients and rounded blob shapes
- Any interface that looks like it could be a software tool for any industry

## Strategic Design Principles

1. **Authority through specificity**: Every copy line should be specific to industrial procurement, never generic. Equipment names, capacity specs, and industry terminology earn trust.
2. **Pricing is a privilege**: Pricing is gated behind registration. The guest experience must feel premium and complete without prices — not broken.
3. **Structured, not social**: This is not a discovery platform. Users arrive with intent. The interface supports deliberate, structured procurement flows, not browsing impulse.
4. **Editorial pacing**: Sections breathe. The light/dark alternation between parchment and near-black creates distinct "rooms" in the journey. No section should feel rushed.
5. **B2B credibility signals**: Client logos, industry names (Taj Hotels, Apollo Hospitals, Indian Navy), 25+ years, ISO certification — these are the trust levers. Use them with restraint, not as noise.
